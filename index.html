Misinformation

Misinformation is spreading false information, whether intentionally or not. It can have very negative consequences on people's safety, health, and reputation, and it can harm them or their families.

In this article we are going to talk about

  1. Misinformation vs Disinformation
  2. Consequences of false information.
  3. Techniques of misinformation.
  4. Prevention and correction of misinformation.

Misinformation vs Disinformation

Misinformation is just spreading false information, meaning the person spreading them may, or may not not they are false.

Disinformation on the other hand strictly means that the person or group spreading these false information know they are false. Their purposes differ. Maybe for personal gain, like attracting attention to their site, or sum financial deal with another group, ideological promotion by harming the other ideologies' reputation. Maybe for their definition of fun or bragging about being able to manipulate people.

Consequences of false information

The Consequences of false information are numerous, can vary according to their type, and can have huge negative impact.

  • Public incitement :Misleading news against a certain group of people based on their religion, race, or idealogy can cause division and tension in society, leading to hatred, violence and crimes and the rise of radical groups.

  • Endangering public or individual health: False information about diseases, medical treatment , or procedures can cause distrust and reject scientific evidence and guidance. This can mislead people into harming themselves and not seeking medical advice when needed.

  • Destroying a person identity: Misinforming may also target an individual, shaming them and ruining their relationships. Destroying someone's character has a devastating impact on the person and may lead them to hurt themselves or others. Maybe this effect needs its own covering since it isn't talked a lot about, and the growth of social media makes hurting someone's emotions very easy.

Techniques of misinformation

Misinformation has different techniques and you may have encountered them before. Here are some:

  • Emotional manipulation: That have different types, using sensational headlines or images, using subjective personal stories and emotive tones for persuasion and making advantage of people in a vulnerable state.

    An example of the latter is taking advantage of people with anxiety disorders. Putting a misleading headline like "Panic attacks can harm you". A title like that scares someone with anxiety. They open the site hoping they find something comforting despite the headline, but the misleader's plan is to scare them more, so they continue browsing and like that the misleader have managed to gain views for this site, despite the scientific evidence and medical professional experts saying the opposite.

  • Deception: Deception involves distorting and manipulating the shape of data to push a narrative or a claim. Some examples are using different scales for data, not highlighting the difference between causality and correlation, small or biased data sampling and using a reference for known figures with quotes they never said.

  • Rumors:A rumor is a story or a statement that got circulated around people without evidence. Rumors can be created around events disguising the truth. sometimes they can even be created from zero. Creating or helping to spread them is a form of misinformation.

Prevention and correction of misinformation

I think it's fair to say this is all responsibility. It can hurt any of us or someone we know, so let's talk about ways to avoid falling for them.

  • Recognize the purpose and source of the information:You have to always think about the purpose of the information you read and its source. Inspect the credibility of whoever is telling them. It's a good attribute to be somewhat skeptical of what you read and not fall for lies or unsupported claims.

  • Understand the techniques and effects of the information: It's a good measure to sometimes read about the techniques and effects of false information like this article. Of course, you can find more info about the subject throughout the internet or books if you are interested.

  • Recognizing our responsibility when sharing and creating information: With the massive growth of social media and the internet, the flow and size of information have followed up. Anyone can share their stories or give info about a subject they know about. Always beware if someone is spreading false information or talking about events that never happen, intentionally or not. Don't be a part of unsupported claims that might hurt someone or a group of people.
